## SECTION: For the Mother  (when you are pouring out)
Field: carrying, holding, compassion, protection, sustaining. Richest well.
Verified distinct terms: 10. Realistic clean ceiling ~10-12 (see ceiling note).

1. nahal    | H5095 | lead gently, at the pace of the weak, to water and rest | Isaiah 40:11 | Not driving a flock forward — leading the ones who can't go fast, to water, to rest.
2. racham   | H7355/H7356 | womb-compassion; a nursing mother's love | Isaiah 49:15 | Same root as rechem (womb). God's mercy = womb-love. "Can a woman forget her nursing child?"
3. aman     | H539  | to support/foster, carry a child | Numbers 11:12 | Root of "amen." The nursing-parent who carries the infant; what holds you up is trustworthy.
4. chazaq   | H2388 | be/grow firm, strong; strengthen | Isaiah 41:10 | Strength that's GIVEN: "I will strengthen you, I will uphold you." Title word.
5. kanaph   | H3671 | wing; corner of a garment that covers | Psalm 91:4 / Ruth 2:12 | Mother-bird wing; chicks run TOWARD it, don't scatter. Pairs w/ chasah (don't reuse that pair).
6. shamar   | H8104 | guard, hedge about, keep watch | Psalm 121 | The keeper who neither slumbers nor sleeps. Up at night = her.
7. nasa     | H5375 | to lift, carry, bear up | Deuteronomy 1:31 | "The LORD carried you as a man carries his son." Same verb lifts a face in blessing (Num 6:26).
8. chabaq   | H2263 | to clasp, embrace, fold in the arms | Genesis 33:4 | USE A PARENTAL/REUNION PASSAGE (Jacob embraced), NOT Song of Songs 2:6 (that's couch-study turf).
9. qabats   | H6908 | to gather in, collect | Isaiah 40:11 | "He gathers the lambs in his arm." The gathering-in motion before the carrying. Distinct from nahal.
10. samak   | H5564 | to prop, uphold the falling, sustain | Psalm 145:14 | "Upholds all who fall, raises the bowed down." Prop under the bent-over mother.

CEILING NOTE: Beyond ~10-12, "Mother" terms start bleeding into refuge (kanaph/chasah)
or strength (chazaq). For entries 11-20, REUSE a verified term above in a fresh
PARENTAL passage rather than forcing a new root. Candidate additional roots if a
clean one is wanted: tamak (H8551, uphold/grasp), chuwl (H2342, writhe/bring forth
in labor) — VERIFY before use, not yet checked.

## SECTION: Stretched Thin  (when there is nothing left)
Field: weariness, depletion, mercy that doesn't run out, being refilled.

1. anapauo  | G373  | to refresh, restore strength — not merely stop | Matthew 11:28 | Intensive of pauo. Same word as God resting on day 7. Promise verb is future active: "I WILL."
2. amal     | H5998/H5999 | toilsome, wearing labor of body and mind | Ecclesiastes (1:3, 2:11) | The draining, often-futile work. The Qoheleth word for being worn out.
3. hesed    | H2617 | covenant loyal-love, steadfast, unfailing | Lamentations 3:22-23 | "New every morning." The depleted woman waking to mercy that didn't run out overnight.
4. emunah   | H530  | firmness, faithfulness, steadfastness | Lamentations 3:23 | "Great is your faithfulness." Distinct noun from the aman root. (verified in Lam 3:22-23 lookup)

CEILING NOTE: this section's well is shallower than Mother. ~12-15 distinct max.
Additional candidates to VERIFY: yagea (H3021, weary/labor-worn), ayeph (H5889,
faint/weary), kashal (H3782, stumble/totter under load).

## SECTION: Permission to Rest  (when you cannot quiet down)
Field: stillness, ceasing, trust, refuge, being held without earning.

1. damam    | H1826 | to be silent, grow still, cease inner clamor | Psalm 131:2 | Paired w/ shavah (Piel, actively calm). The weaned child who stopped reaching. Stillness you choose.
2. chasah   | H2620 | to flee for protection, take refuge, confide in | Psalm 34:8 | USE A NON-kanaph PASSAGE (taste-and-see) to avoid overlap with Mother's Ps 91:4/Ruth 2:12.
3. batach   | H982  | to trust, lean confidently, be secure | Isaiah 26:3 / Psalm 37 | "Perfect peace, mind stayed on you." Distinct from chasah — confidence/leaning, not fleeing.

CEILING NOTE: Rest is the SHALLOWEST well — likely the first to hit reuse. ~10-12 distinct.
"Rest" near-synonyms cluster fast. Additional to VERIFY: nuach (H5117, rest/settle),
shaqat (H8252, be quiet/at ease), shabath (H7673, cease/sabbath), raga (H7280, be at rest).

## SECTION: The Words You Carry  (when the words run out — self-talk)
Field: the tongue turned inward, the voice she uses on herself, finding her voice.

1. lashon   | H3956 | the tongue, and the speech it carries | Proverbs 18:21 | "Life and death in the hand of the tongue." Verse never specified an audience. The inner voice holds the tool too.

CEILING NOTE: this is the THINNEST section as scoped (inward self-talk). To reach
20, widen the field to: words that build (Eph 4:29), the soft answer (Prov 15:1),
the heart's overflow into speech (Luke 6:45), naming/calling (qara), the still
small voice (1 Kings 19:12). VERIFY each. Keep it INWARD (self-talk), not
marriage-communication — that ground belongs to the Campbell Covenant couch study.
